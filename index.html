Introduction to Leopard Geckos

Leopard geckos are small, nocturnal, ground-dwelling lizards native to the rocky grasslands and deserts of Afghanistan, Iran, Pakistan, India, and Nepal. They are popular pets due to their docile nature and relatively simple care requirements.

Housing and Enclosure Setup

A 10-20 gallon tank is sufficient for a single leopard gecko. The enclosure should have a secure lid and adequate ventilation. Provide a temperature gradient with a warm side (88-92°F) and a cool side (75-80°F). A heat mat is a good source of belly heat. Include at least two hides (one warm, one cool) and a moist hide to aid in shedding. Use a substrate like paper towels, reptile carpet, or slate tiles. Avoid sand, as it can cause impaction.

Diet and Feeding

Leopard geckos are insectivores. Their diet should consist of crickets, mealworms, and dubia roaches. Insects should be gut-loaded with nutritious food before being offered to the gecko. Dust insects with a calcium supplement with D3 at every other feeding and a multivitamin supplement once a week. Fresh, clean water should always be available in a shallow dish.

Health and Wellness

Common health issues include metabolic bone disease (MBD), impaction, and respiratory infections. MBD is caused by a lack of calcium and vitamin D3. Impaction is often caused by ingesting loose substrate. Respiratory infections can be caused by low temperatures or high humidity. Regular veterinary check-ups are recommended.
